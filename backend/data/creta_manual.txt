8. Emergency Situations While Driving

Hazard Warning Flasher
1. Switch on the hazard warning flasher immediately to warn other road users.
2. Use the hazard warning lights whenever the vehicle is stopped in an unsafe location or during an emergency.

If the Engine Stalls While Driving
1. Reduce vehicle speed gradually.
2. Keep the steering wheel under control.
3. Move the vehicle to the side of the road as soon as it is safe.
4. Switch on the hazard warning flasher.

If the Engine Stalls at a Crossroad or Crossing
1. Attempt to move the vehicle out of traffic if possible.
2. Switch on the hazard warning flasher immediately.
3. Seek assistance if the vehicle cannot be moved safely.

If You Have a Flat Tyre While Driving
1. Reduce speed slowly and avoid sudden braking.
2. Hold the steering wheel firmly.
3. Move the vehicle to a safe place away from traffic.
4. Switch on the hazard warning flasher.
5. Inspect the tyre only after the vehicle is safely stopped.

If the Engine Does Not Start
1. Check that the gear position is correct.
2. Check battery connections for looseness or corrosion.
3. Attempt to start the vehicle again.
4. If required, use proper jump-starting procedures.
5. Seek professional assistance if the engine does not start.

Jump Starting the Vehicle
1. Ensure both vehicles are switched off before connecting cables.
2. Connect jumper cables in the correct sequence.
3. Start the assisting vehicle first.
4. Attempt to start the stalled vehicle.
5. Disconnect the cables carefully after the vehicle starts.

If the Engine Overheats
1. Stop the vehicle immediately in a safe location.
2. Switch off the engine.
3. Allow the engine to cool down before opening the bonnet.
4. Do not open the radiator cap while the engine is hot.
5. Check coolant level only after the engine has cooled.
6. Seek professional assistance if overheating continues.

If the Vehicle Must Be Stopped on the Road
1. Move the vehicle as far off the road as possible.
2. Switch on the hazard warning flasher.
3. Place a warning triangle if available.
4. Keep occupants away from traffic lanes.


Pre-drive check
Before starting the vehicle for a long drive, ensure the battery is in good condition and the terminals are secure.
Check that all warning indicators on the instrument cluster turn off after starting.
Verify that the fuel level is sufficient for the planned journey.
Ensure that the engine oil and coolant levels are within the recommended range.
